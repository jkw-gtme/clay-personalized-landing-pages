The Problem

GTM engineers are stuck
maintaining, not building.

01

Every new data source is another engineering project

Adding a data provider means a new API contract, new authentication, new schema mapping, and a new pipeline to maintain. What should take a day takes a sprint — and then needs monitoring forever.

02

Ops is bottlenecked on your ticket queue

RevOps and sales teams can't iterate on enrichment logic without filing a ticket. Business experiments that should take hours get queued behind infrastructure work — and momentum dies in the backlog.

03

Fragile pipelines break at the worst moments

Provider APIs change, response formats shift, rate limits get hit mid-campaign. Someone has to be on-call for enrichment infrastructure — and that someone is usually you.

04

Custom enrichment logic is technical debt from day one

Internal enrichment scripts accumulate edge cases, hardcoded logic, and undocumented dependencies. When you leave or get pulled to other work, no one else can maintain them.

How Clay Works

Ship enrichment workflows
in hours, not sprints.

Clay is the GTM development environment that lets you — and your ops team — build, iterate, and scale data workflows without writing or maintaining a line of infrastructure code.

01

150+ Providers, One Interface

Apollo, ZoomInfo, LinkedIn, Clearbit, and 147 more — all accessible through Clay's no-code interface. No API keys to manage, no rate limits to babysit, no contracts to juggle.

02

Waterfall Logic Built-In

Define a fallback chain across providers in minutes. Clay handles the sequencing, deduplication, and failure recovery automatically — no custom retry logic required.

03

When no provider has the signal you need, Claygent scrapes websites, reads LinkedIn profiles, and summarizes news — then outputs structured data your CRM can ingest.

04

Native Push to Every Tool

Enriched data flows directly to Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach, or Salesloft. No custom connectors, no middleware, no glue code — ops owns the destination logic.
